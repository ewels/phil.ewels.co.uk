Dr Philip Ewels - Curriculum Vitae

To get in touch, please use the contact form to e-mail me.

Education & Experience

  • Bioinformatician - Science for Life Laboratory, Stockholm, Sweden (2014 - present)
    • I work in the Genomics Applications facility at SciLifeLab, writing new pipelines and analysis methodologies for research groups across Sweden.
  • Bioinformatics Postdoctoral Research Scientist - Babraham Institute, Cambridge, UK (2012 – 2014)
    • Bioinformatics postdoctoral position in the group of Professor Wolf Reik FRS. The group studies the epigenetic regulation of organisms. My role included computational processing and of RNA-Sequencing data, Bisulfite Methylation data, Hi-C spatial data and other types of next-generation sequencing experiments. I was involved in multiple studies, including the analysis of methylation patterns and machinery in social insects; changes in the regulation and organisation of chromatin in senescent fibroblast nuclei. In addition to project work, I developed bioinformatics tools for general use.
  • Postdoctoral Research Associate - Trinity Hall, Cambridge (2012 – 2014)
    • A competitive position, giving membership to Trinity Hall and the University of Cambridge. Involved in teaching and support for graduate students as well as frequent talks within a college lecture series.
  • PhD in Molecular Biology - Babraham Institute, University of Cambridge, (2008 – 2012)
    • Supervisor: Dr Cameron Osborne. Thesis title: “Spatial organisation of proto-oncogenes in human haematopoietic progenitor cells.” I investigated the physical organisation of DNA and how gene associations may lead to specific translocations. I used many molecular biology techniques and developed the ‘enriched 4C’ technique.
  • Scientific Language Editing - European Physics Journal (2008 – 2009)
    • Correcting the grammar and style of scientific papers, pre-publication, on behalf of the EPJ family of journals for non-English speaking authors.
  • BSc in Biochemistry With Study In Industry - University of Bristol (2004 – 2008)
    • Awarded first class honours, with the highest mark of my year. Spent one year working at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute in Cambridge. In my final year lab project, I worked with Dr P Cullen studying the role of Sorting Nexin 1 in Salmonella invasion and endosomal sorting.
  • Industrial Placement - Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute (2006 - 2007)
    • Industrial placement year, working under the supervision of Dr Panos Deloukas. I ran and maintained the Sequenom MassArray genotyping platform. My work included troubleshooting problems, analysis of results and improving workflow. The work resulted in a number of publications.
  • Web Design - Customised Web (2002 - present)
    • I am a professional website designer in my spare time. I specialise in creating websites for scientific projects and start-up companies. I trade as a UK sole trader under the name Customised Web. I recently redesigned the Babraham Institute website. See customisedweb.com for more information.
  • Shrewsbury Sixth Form College (2002 – 2004)
    • A2 levels: Chemistry (A), Biology (A), Physics (A), Maths (A)

Prizes / Awards

  • Science, Engineering and Technology Student of the Year - Biology and Biotechnology (2008)
    • National award, judged by the Institute of Biology and a representative from AstraZeneca. I was awarded this prize for work carried out whilst studying at the University of Bristol.
  • William Edward Garner Prize (2008)
    • Prize for the highest degree marks from a year of 69 biochemistry students at the University of Bristol.

Skills

  • Bioinformatics
    • Whilst at the Babraham Institute I have been involved in the development of several bioinformatics tools including FastQC (next-generation sequencing data quality control), Bismark (bisulfite methylation analysis tool) and HiCUP (Hi-C data processing pipeline). I have headed up two projects of my own: Labrador, a web based tool to find and access data processed by the Bioinformatics group; Cluster Flow, a pipeline package to automate and standardise common processing steps using our GRIDEngine cluster, written in Perl. I have released some tools for public use: http://www.tallphil.co.uk/bioinformatics
  • Coding Experience
    • I am proficient in the use of Perl and R for the analysis of biological data. I have used BioPerl and Bioconductor and am familiar with packages such as DE-Seq and Diffbind. I have some experience of Python and am currently learning to code in Java. I routinely use version control systems such as SVN and Git. I am experienced in web development code, including PHP, Javascript, HTML and CSS. I am comfortable using Unix/Linux environments with command line control, as well as OSX and Windows systems.
  • Molecular Biology
    • Experienced in many techniques including cell culture, DNA extraction and purification, cloning, FACS, 3C based assays (chromatin conformation capture), PCR, preparation of next gen sequencing libraries.
  • Teaching
    • I have been involved in the development and presentation of a number of bioinformatics training courses at the Babraham Institute and University of Cambridge including topics such as SeqMonk, R and Perl. I have written and delivered an institute training course for my software, Labrador.
  • Leadership
    • My skills in group leadership are reflected in the positions that I hold in my social life, for example the elected positions of Boat Club Captain and Graduate Computing Officer.
  • Presentation
    • My PhD has required me to present my data in a number of talks. Senior members of staff have remarked that my presenting style is confident and clear. I have also presented my research in poster sessions at a number of events, including the 2011 BBSRC annual conference in Norwich.
  • Project Management
    • I am skilled at managing targets and deadlines, a necessity for my PhD which requires a great deal of independence and motivation. I am in charge of my work and responsible for organising my time.

    • I am a keen climber and love the outdoors. I often spend my summers mountaineering in the French Alps and have recently taken up ice-climbing. I commute around 30 km to and from work on my bike daily and have entered a number of cycling sportive events.
